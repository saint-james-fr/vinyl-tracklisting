Technical Informations

  • The first thing to know is that the more music there is on one side, the less dynamic and therefore, we approach the surface noise.
  • On a 12inch, ideally, it should not exceed 22 minutes at 33 RPM 1/3 and 10 minutes at 45 RPM.
  • Ideally, you should place the energetic tracks on the beginning of the side of the disc, and the quieter pieces towards the end of the side.
  • The more stereo there is, the more space the groove will take.
  • You can burn mono music louder than stereo music.
  • Quieter tracks take up less space than heavy tracks.
  • High frequencies are more difficult to engrave.
  • Too high high frequencies cause saturation during playback.
  • Do not push the stereo to low frequencies.
  • Apply a strong deesser to the vocals.
  • Do not compress the track too much- it is better for the cut (DR 14 decibels).
  • Think from the start of production to work in high resolution (minimum 24 bits/ 48 kHz).
